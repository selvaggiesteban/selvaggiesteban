<p><strong>Objective:</strong>&nbsp;this guide shows you step by step how to access the WordPress panel, identify the main parts of the dashboard, and configure the basic general settings of your site.</p>



<figure class="aligncenter"><img width="1024" height="576" src="/assets/blog/guia-wordpress-1.webp" alt=""></figure>



<h2>1. Prerequisites</h2>



<ul>
<li>An up-to-date web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, etc.).</li>



<li>Internet connection.</li>



<li>Access URL to the WordPress panel (usually ends in&nbsp;<code>/wp-admin</code>).</li>



<li>WordPress username and password provided by the administrator.</li>
</ul>



<h2>2. How to access the WordPress panel</h2>



<h3>2.1 Open the access URL</h3>



<ol>
<li>Open your browser.</li>



<li>Enter the access URL in the address bar, for example:&nbsp;<code>https://yoursite.com/wp-admin</code>.</li>



<li>Press&nbsp;<strong>Enter</strong>&nbsp;to load the login page.</li>
</ol>



<figure class="aligncenter"><img width="1024" height="576" src="/assets/blog/guia-wordpress-2.webp" alt=""></figure>



<h3>2.2 Enter your username and password</h3>



<ol>
<li>In the&nbsp;<strong>Username or Email Address</strong>&nbsp;field, enter your username.</li>



<li>In the&nbsp;<strong>Password</strong>&nbsp;field, enter the assigned password.</li>



<li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>Log In</strong>&nbsp;button.</li>
</ol>



<p>If the credentials are correct, you will access the&nbsp;<strong>WordPress Dashboard</strong>.</p>



<h3>2.3 Common login errors</h3>



<ul>
<li><strong>Incorrect password:</strong>&nbsp;check capitalization and keyboard language.</li>



<li><strong>Non-existent user:</strong>&nbsp;verify that you have entered the correct username or email.</li>



<li><strong>Blank screen or server error:</strong>&nbsp;this may be a hosting issue; in that case, contact support.</li>
</ul>

<h2>4. Accessing the general site settings</h2>



<h3>4.1 Locate the "Settings > General" option</h3>



<ol>
<li>From the sidebar menu, scroll down to the bottom.</li>



<li>Click on&nbsp;<strong>Settings</strong>.</li>



<li>Select the&nbsp;<strong>General</strong>&nbsp;option.</li>
</ol>



<figure class="aligncenter"><img width="1024" height="576" src="/assets/blog/guia-wordpress-11.webp" alt=""></figure>



<h3>4.2 Main fields of the general settings</h3>



<h4>Site Title</h4>



<p>Your website's main name. It usually appears in the browser tab and in theme areas. Example: "Example Store" or "Company Name".</p>



<h4>Tagline</h4>



<p>A brief phrase that complements the title, for example: "Online store for home products".</p>



<h4>WordPress Address (URL) and Site Address (URL)</h4>



<p>Fields that indicate where WordPress is installed and which address is used to access the site.</p>



<p><strong>It is not recommended to modify them</strong>&nbsp;unless explicitly instructed, as you could make the site inaccessible.</p>



<h4>Administration Email Address</h4>



<p>The email that receives important system notifications (new users, critical changes, etc.). It should be an inbox that is checked frequently.</p>



<h4>Membership and Default Role</h4>



<p>If the option for anyone to register is enabled, this is where you define the default role (e.g., Subscriber). If you are unsure, the usual practice is to disable open registration.</p>



<h4>Timezone</h4>



<p>Allows you to define the site's local time. The most recommended option is to choose a reference city (e.g., "Madrid" or "Buenos Aires").</p>



<h4>Date Format and Time Format</h4>



<p>Defines how dates and times are displayed on the website. Choose the format commonly used in your country.</p>



<h4>Week Starts On</h4>



<p>Used in calendars and widgets. In many countries it is Monday.</p>



<h2>5. Saving changes and reviewing the site</h2>



<h3>5.1 Save changes</h3>



<ol>
<li>When you finish reviewing and adjusting the fields, scroll down to the bottom of the page.</li>



<li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>Save Changes</strong>&nbsp;button.</li>
</ol>



<figure class="aligncenter"><img width="1024" height="576" src="/assets/blog/guia-wordpress-12.webp" alt=""></figure>



<h3>5.2 Verify that everything works</h3>



<ol>
<li>Open your site in a new tab.</li>



<li>Check that the title, description, and language display correctly.</li>



<li>If you changed the timezone, verify that post and comment dates are consistent.</li>
</ol>



<h2>6. Managing users and logging out</h2>



<h3>6.1 View the list of users</h3>



<p>From the sidebar menu, go to&nbsp;<strong>Users > All Users</strong>&nbsp;to see who has access to the panel.</p>



<figure class="aligncenter"><img width="1024" height="576" src="/assets/blog/guia-wordpress-13.webp" alt=""></figure>



<h3>6.2 Log out</h3>



<ol>
<li>In the top right corner, click on your username.</li>



<li>Select the&nbsp;<strong>Log Out</strong>&nbsp;option.</li>



<li>WordPress will take you back to the login screen.</li>
</ol>



<p>If the system asks for your username and password again when you return, the session has been closed successfully.</p>
